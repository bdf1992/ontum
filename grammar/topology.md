# The topology axis — how the grammar adapts to a target

## Pin

The 8 systems × 8 pointers are **root behaviors** — the common patterns
and actions of *any* workplace, not a spoken language. No one speaks this
grammar; it is a **meta-grammar that adapts to a target system**.

You don't *use* the grammar directly. You **relate a target bounded
environment to it**: AI inference workflows + deterministic tools map the
target system onto the root behaviors, and the placement **configures the
setpoints** of that environment and its workflow (the `Settings`/`ADMIT`
system). The grammar is the fixed alphabet; a target environment is a
*projection* onto it.

This makes the grammar an **internal topology** — and the topological
sections describe *how each behavior relates to the environment's
boundary.*

## The three axes (= your cube)

Your six labels are **three opposite pairs** — three axes — which is
exactly a cube (6 faces, 3 axes). This resolves the earlier open
question: **each cube face is one topological pole, and each face carries
the full 8×8 grammar.** The cube is the grammar projected through
topology.

| axis | poles | what it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Region** | `Internal` ↔ `External` | inside vs outside the boundary |
| **Flow** | `Ingress` ↔ `Egress` | crossing in vs crossing out |
| **Reach** | `Local` ↔ `Global` | this scope vs across scopes |

6 poles → 6 faces. (The 8 corners = the 2³ combinations of the three
axes — e.g. *internal·ingress·local* — an octant. Open whether that's the
right reading of the corners.)

## The section your list omits — and it's load-bearing

**The Boundary (∂) — the seam / membrane / fence itself.** Internal and
External are two *regions*; the Boundary is the third, and it is where
everything happens: ingress/egress *cross* it, the gate *sits on* it,
local/global are *measured from* it. It is not a fourth pair — it is the
thing all three axes are defined against. It is your earlier **membrane**
("between a reality and its basis") and the **fence** ("closed loop round
a territory"). On the cube it is the **center**, not a face.

## Grounding — this topology already runs in the repo

The sections are not invented; they map onto real machinery:

| section | real machinery |
|---|---|
| **Boundary** | `fence/policy.py` — the membrane around the territory |
