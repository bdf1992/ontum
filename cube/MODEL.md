# CNEX model — the metabolized spec (living; refine as bdo corrects)

Status: **synthesis** from bdo's corrections (2026-06-22/23). Not final; the
geometry is being iterated. This is the durable home for the model so it is
not re-derived each session.

## Dimensions

- region, flow, reach (and any others) are each an **independent dimension**
  in one **shared space**. An element *placed* in the space is read on every
  dimension at once.
- Dimensions do **not** affect each other unless a **coupling / entanglement /
  nesting is explicitly named and intentional**. (A point being read on all
  axes is NOT the axes being coupled — keep them distinct.)
- ingress / egress is the **direction of travel** along a dimension (in vs
  out), not a separate axis.

## The neck

- **Fixed at center.** You never move the neck; you offset the **backdrop**
  (the field/frame) from standard, which is how a bounded region lands
  off-center.
- The neck is itself a **bounded structure** with 6 faces, 12 edges, 8
  corners. The **triads are the 8 corners of the neck** (where three neck
  faces meet) — attached to the neck, NOT flung to the external ±1 axis.
- **inversion** through the neck **reconstructs** the structure (not a mirror).

## Bounded regions / setpoints

- A **setpoint is a bounded WINDOW, never a point**: horizontal center +
  selection width + signed vertical bounds, authored at a projection angle.
- The window is the **acceptable tolerance area**. A current value inside →
  **accepted**; outside → **rejected**. Move = retarget; resize = re-tolerance.
- Offset / asymmetric bounds are allowed.
- **internal / external are relative** — frame- and reach-driven against
  whichever bounded region is in play. The same location can be internal to
  one bound and external to another; naming the frame resolves the apparent
  paradox.

## Fractal nesting

- Every neck **contains another whole bounded structure one decimal finer**
  (inward); outward, **past +1 is +10** (the next coarser scale). Zoom both
  ways → structures within structures.

## Dynamics

- **Live, interactive, reactive** — not a static notation.
- The system's **grammar** assigns elements impact / weight / stats /
  settings, and those **move the regions when used, by anticipating
  consequences** (ties to the repo's consequence-policy model).

## reach

- reach is about **travel, inversion, and moving through gradients**; its
  meaning (like internal/external) is **relative to your boundary**.

## The cube, faces, edges (settled)

- The **cone ends are capped with planes → the bounding cube.** The 6 planes
  are the **faces** — the "another layer of topology" where concepts / code /
  systems get placed. Each face carries a **portal / threshold** (the open
  inlet of its cone).
- A **cone funnels from each face portal inward to the neck/gateway.** The
  cone is open (an inlet); the neck is the gateway where all funnels converge.
- The **12 edges** are the **intersection loci** where the 3 dual-cone volumes
  cross and bind against the planes. Rendered as **braided / Möbius edges**
  (the "tori" were a stand-in; the 12-edge structure is the real thing).
- The cones **bore holes through the inner (gateway) cube**, and this
  hole/portal pattern **recurs along the cone, inward and outward** (the
  decimal nesting: +1→+10 out, finer scale in). Today: one inward level
  (gateway holes). Full fractal recursion + the true computed intersection
  geometry are the next build.

## Travel vs recurse (pondering 2026-06-23 — promising, NOT settled)

A split that "could make lots of sense" (bdo, late-night) — capture, don't
treat as law yet:

- **Gateway = travel** (move within a scale): in a portal → cone throat →
  gateway hub → out another portal. The **bored-tunnel** torus.
- **Triad seams = recurse** (move between scales): through the **corners**.
  This gives the 12 **edges** a job — they're the seams/conduits to the
  corners. The **wrap/gluing** torus.
- So both torus readings are true, one per operation: tunnels = travel,
  wrap-seams = recursion.
- **Outside the cube = the next scale up**, reached through the same seams; and
  **each boundary crossing inverts you** (the neck inversion echoed at every
  scale boundary). Symmetric in/out: seams inward → a scale down, seams
  outward → a scale up.

Open within this:
- Direction: does **out-through-a-corner = up a scale** (+1→+10) and **in = down**?
- Travel routing: always through the gateway hub, or also face-to-face?
