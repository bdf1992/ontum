# The explore lobe — research over priors that pays out in pointed calls to action

> **Provisional name.** The organ is real; the title is bdo's to mint.
> **Status: PROPOSED** — captured from the design conversation of 2026-06-19.
> It *holds* the open forks; it resolves none of them. Capturing is not
> deciding (the whole point of this organ is to not collapse early).

## The frame, in the order it arrived

1. **The shared enemy.** Math, research, science, art, discovery, reporting,
   news, sharing, communication, hunting — every one of them treats
   uncertainty as an enemy: a force to be cut down into a shape you can
   defend against and prepare for. They are **uncertainty-terminators**.

2. **Ontum is built entirely out of terminators.** A gate cuts (yes/no/amend).
   A fold collapses (the digest reduces a span to a verdict). `term_economy`
   forces every term into exactly one class. The grip discipline —
   *"no evidence, no mint"* — is the purest one: a machine for refusing the
   uncertain. The owner's stamp is the final cut. The system has a fully-built
   **convergent (exploit) lobe** and **no divergent (explore) lobe.**

3. **This organ is the missing lobe.** It does the one move everything else
   refuses: it *holds the volume open* — research over priors — instead of
   cutting it down. It lives in the room the grip discipline left empty on
   purpose: `proposed` / `projected` / `poetic` are the *uncollapsed* states,
   today treated as transit toward `minted`. Here they are **home, not transit.**
   It never *claims*; it only *holds*. So it does not break the grip discipline —
   it inhabits its negative space.

4. **The payout is not capital.** The first reflex was to make the organ
   *capitalize* research into production value. That smuggles the enemy back in
   through the goal: capital is the future closed and banked — the most
   aggressive terminator there is. **Capital is one point of one axis.**

5. **The payout is a call to action, given a purpose and a goal.** A call to
   action does not terminate uncertainty — it engages it. "Given where this
   might be heading, and what we're after, here is the move worth making." It
   pays out in **motion, not storage**: the draw that never spends the
   principal. You act *into* the uncertainty; the volume stays whole.

6. **The goal is a direction in a manifold, not a payoff.** The organ is
   **axis-agnostic.** The opening list was the axes: truth (math/science),
   inquiry (research/discovery), expression (art), transmission
   (reporting/news/sharing/communication), provisioning (hunting) — and yes,
   economic (capital) is one more. "Given a purpose and a goal" = a *direction*
   in that manifold. The organ researches the priors and emits the move that
   travels whatever direction it is pointed.

## The organ, in one line

**Pointed at a purpose+goal (a direction in the value-manifold), it researches
over priors and emits a call to action — marked, proposed-tier, never minted —
that moves the system toward that direction without closing the future.**

It is the **forward-looking twin of `gaps.py`**:

- `gaps.py` / `summon` emit calls to action from the **certain present** (a mock
  stage, a parked atom). The *maintenance backlog* — the exploit lobe's CTA.
- This organ emits calls to action from **research over priors toward a goal**.
  The *opportunity frontier* — the explore lobe's CTA.

Same output shape (a move worth making); opposite lobe feeding it.

## What it is NOT

- **Not a persona / voice.** It is an organ with a characteristic output shape,
  not a character.
- **Not a predictor or rumor-mill that puts uncertainty on trial.** A forecast
  scored guilty/innocent is the terminator reflex. This pays out in action, not
  verdicts.
- **Not a capitalizer.** It does not book value. It moves the system.
- **Not a second source of truth.** Its calls to action are marked conjecture;
  verdicts still land only through the existing pens (D-4).

## The open forks (unresolved on purpose)

1. **Handed vs self-aiming.** Does the organ get *handed* its purpose+goal (you
   point it at an epic's horizon / an outcome), or does it *find* its own — read
   the field and decide what is worth a call to action without being aimed?
2. **The axes.** Are the ten roughly the axes — or are they points, and the real
   axes are something underneath them?
3. **Spoken vs written.** A call to action is motion, not storage — but does the
   motion leave a trace? A marked `conjecture` ledger lets a call-to-action be
   looked back on (did acting on it pay?), at the cost of a new ledger. Or it
   stays spoken — surfaced now, never persisted.
