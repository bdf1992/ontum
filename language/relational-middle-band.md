## Pin

The model-free / model-based **binary is wrong**. The honest structure is a
**three-band spectrum** — `raw → relational → mechanistic` — and "smart
mashing" is the *middle* band: **representation without mechanism**. A
relational act has collapsed a chaotic input space into equivalence classes
("situations that respond the same way") and acts *within a class*; it
carries meaning without matter. That is neither raw (it is not pressing
buttons in the unstructured input space) nor mechanistic (it has no causal
"why"). It is a third thing, between the two the binary admits.

The load-bearing consequence: **learning-progress must be measured over
relation buckets, not undifferentiated action space.** A learning signal
computed over raw actions sees motion and calls it progress
("staring at static"); a signal computed over relation buckets sees whether
the equivalence classes are *stabilizing into meaningful collisions*. The
metric's denominator changes from "actions taken" to "relations
discriminated."

This is the finding stated verbatim by the atom that carries it:
"Smart mashing is not raw model-free action but a relational middle band
(representation without mechanism), so the model-free to model-based axis
becomes raw to relational to mechanistic and learning-progress must be
measured over relation buckets, not undifferentiated action space"

Read against the formality gradient

end (use decided entirely by feedback, no settled form), mechanistic

`language/semantics.md` already locates meaning: "a relation across a gap,
the seam between two decided forms" (`language/strata.md:42`–`43`). The
binary collapses that edge to a point; the spectrum restores it.

The measurement claim lands on semantics' own open hole. `semantics.md`
holds the metric as "the stratum's load-bearing hole … manufactured —

that "a leakage *number* without an admitted metric reports obstruction

over relation buckets is a *candidate filling* of that hole: a metric whose
unit is the relation, admitted before it is trusted (the same caution
`semantics.md` already keeps).

## Non-example

A learning-progress signal computed over **undifferentiated action space**
is the non-example — and it is the precise thing the correction refuses. It
looks locally fine: it produces a number, it rises and falls, it can be
plotted. But it cannot tell "the masher is discovering new equivalence
classes" from "the masher is repeating the same class faster," because its
denominator never distinguished the two. It is the `semantics.md` warning
made concrete: a number without the right metric "reports obstruction where
there is none" (`language/semantics.md:35`–`36`). The bucket is what makes
the signal mean something; remove it and the signal is staring at static.

A second non-example, for the band itself: calling any non-mechanistic act
"raw." That is the binary's error. A relational act has *structure* (the
equivalence classes); flattening it to "raw" is exactly the collapse this
file exists to undo.
