## Pin (of the frame, not the answer)

Pragmatics is meaning in use: what an utterance does between
speakers, in context. Ontum's speakers are the two parent registers —
deterministic code and model inference — and its site of use is the
seam where they negotiate. The candidate shape of an ontum
"utterance" is the cycle: a source event traversing
SUBJECT → … → GATE, certified by the Machine's three verdicts
(`docs/phase-2/ontogrammatic-systems.md:183`). The third verdict is
the pragmatic move proper: when a check cannot be evaluated, *needs
instrumentation* turns the gap into a typed source event — "gaps in
the discipline become the discipline extending itself"
(`ontogrammatic-systems.md:189`). A language that grows exactly where
it fails to speak is alive; that aliveness is a pragmatic property,
not a syntactic or semantic one.

## Creole reading

A creole stays a creole by being used; unused, it fossilizes into a
cipher (`docs/sources/rosetta-creole.md`, cited as inspiration). The
source's loop has no gate, and the vault names ungated
self-obligation as a failure mode — so the recorded direction stands:
**self-extension proposes, the owner admits** (D-4). The substrate's
`needs-you` and the Machine's `needs instrumentation` are different
verdicts; the join — a self-feeding channel that still passes through
bdo's stamp — is better than either half.

## Holdings (real on disk today)

- The loop substrate is the only running pragmatics: verdicts are
  real acts on the record (receipts on the log), seams between
  sessions and the owner are where work actually negotiates. The
  substrate is the harness, never the building (report 0004) — but it
  is also the language's first speech community, and that is worth
  recording.
- Nothing of the fabric's own pragmatics is built: no emitter, no
  drift measurement, no instrumentation verdict.

## The falsifiable test (named, not built)

The agent-context emitter: emit context from the fabric, hand it to a
model, measure drift against raw prose. The first check of the fabric
(not the substrate) that could actually fail — which is what would
make this stratum real rather than described.

## Non-example

Documentation about ontum is not its pragmatics. Describing use is
not use. Pragmatics accrues only when a speaker — human or model —
acts *through* the language and the seam records what happened.
