## The process (recursive pilish v0)

Pilish threads word-lengths along π's digits — an external oracle. Recursive
pilish replaces the oracle with the structure itself, at two nested scales:

1. **The constraint stream is the letter.** For each of the 26 letters, fill
   a 27-slot frame with English words bearing that initial. The initial is
   the digit; the frame is the line of verse. Survey, never mint: the
   lexifier donates, the geometry will place.
2. **The slot census is the cube's own component count.** 27 slots per frame
   = 8 corner-grade + 12 edge-grade + 6 face-grade + 1 center. Kind-tagging
   is deferred to the placement pass; the basin only fills slots.
3. **The center is the frame's wildcard.** One word per frame is flagged ⊙ —
   the pivot candidate, the lens the other 26 should be facets of. It is the
   frame-local image of ⊘.
4. **The recursion clause.** Each frame's center is simultaneously one cell
   of the dimension above. The dimension above raw letters is the cube
   itself — so the **void's frame is exempt from the initial constraint**
   (the wildcard escapes the Pilish rule, exactly as the open axis escapes
   the sign) and is filled instead with the cube's own component vocabulary:
   **the basis, encoded as glyphs.** One level up, the whole cube is a single
   point in its parent; the same exemption will apply there.
5. **Holes are data.** Where the lexifier runs dry, slots stay OPEN — never
   force junk. Sparse letters are not failures; see the finding at the end.

## v1 amendment — the graded composition contract

*Status: laws DERIVED (verified computationally over all 27 addresses);
the canonical utterance order and the falsifier's application are MINTED.*

The flat census 8/12/6/1 under-specifies the frame. The addresses carry two
incidence fans, dual to each other:

| law | direction | rule | sizes (corner·edge·face·center) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **closure** ↓ | composition (boundary + self) | each open axis closes 3 ways: **\|↓g\| = 3^dim** | 1 · 3 · 9 · 27 |
| **star** ↑ | request (cascade + self) | each decided axis opens 2 ways: **\|↑g\| = 2^codim** | 8 · 4 · 2 · 1 |

Composition reads downward in base 3; request reads upward in base 2. Each
cell's closure is itself a `{−,0,+}^dim` ternary lattice — an edge contains a
3-cell line, a face a 9-cell square, the body the whole cube: the self-mapping
is graded and already present in the address algebra. Closure descent
**27→9→3→1 derives Hypothesis A** from incidence (survey doc open item 4:
closeable).

**What the process inherits (pilish v1):**

1. **Graded utterance.** Speaking a glyph in full = speaking its closure,
   3^dim words, boundary first, self last (order provisional):
   S in full is *E·F·S*; W in full is nine words; ⊘ in full is the alphabet.
   Utterance length reveals dimension.
2. **Composition is reference, not vocabulary.** A face's 4 points + 4 edges
   are glyphs the frame already holds; no boundary slot mints a new word.
3. **The placement falsifier.** A word placed at a cell must read as composed
   of its ↓ glyphs, or it is misplaced — refusal with a receipt. (Applies at
   placement, not at survey: the basin stays dirty.)
4. **Graded census.** Σ 3^dim over the 27 cells = **125 = 5³** = (2+3)³:
   each axis decided two ways or open three. One letter's fully-graded room
   has 125 slot-references over its 27 words.

**Fence:** the viewer's neighbor counts (7/11/17/26) are *grid adjacency*
(unit distance in the 3×3×3), not incidence. The chip/openness gradings ride
the grid; the composition law rides the poset. Two adjacency columns, kept
apart.

### The correction

The earlier ⊘ section conflated two levels. The cube-component vocabulary
(Corner, Edge, Face, Vertex, Origin…) is the **parent dimension's frame** —
the cube-above-letters, per the recursion clause. It keeps its placements
(autological law: a geometric term sits at the kind it names; Origin pinned
to `(−,−,−)`; duals antipodal unless self-description overrides — a measured
precedence). It is hereby relabeled **the parent frame**, not ⊘'s own.

**⊘'s own frame** — the center cube receiving its own 27 — is the
**interstitial frame**: the distinctions hiding inside the undifferentiated
word "center," and the membranes of the seams.

### The interstitial 27

| kind | terms | logic |
|---|---|---|
| 8 corners | Centroid · Barycenter · Keystone · Spindle · Midpoint · Fulcrum · Focus · Hub | determinate centers — eight fully-decided ways of being the middle (frame, mass, correction, mechanism, metric, force, convergence, traffic) |
| 12 edges | Cant · Offset · Displacement · Hinge · Joint · Suture · Weld · Bridge · Valve · Pore · Threshold · Gasket | corrections and crossings between centers; each an edge whose poles are two determinate centers |
| 6 faces | Membrane · Interface · Meniscus · Septum · Film · Lamina | the interstitial surfaces — where negotiation has area |
| center ⊙ | **Interstice** | the lens: all 26 are ways of being-between |

**Derived placement, worked:** Cant is the edge whose poles are Centroid and

non-example for Cant ("barycenter coincident with the keystone") is exactly
this edge degenerating to a point: the ledger's falsifier was geometric all
along. Drift lives in Cant's star (one axis opened: the trajectory above the
displacement). The other 11 edge pole-pairs: OPEN.

### The descent law [MINTED]

**A frame's center names what the frame cannot distinguish; descending into
it mints the distinctions.** The parent says "center"; the child says
twenty-seven kinds of middle. Corollary: collisions in a frame are not
synonym welds to canonicalize — they are words demanding the finer frame one
level down. Descent disambiguates. Prediction it makes: S's child-frame
(descent through "Seam") should partially share cells with the interstitial
frame, and that overlap is itself a seam between frames — testable by
drafting S's descent and diffing.
