# Semiotics — the signs themselves

*Mark (proposed): **Y**, the face `(0,0,−)` — one decided axis made
visible, organizing four seams (the knolling: Y is seam of M, O, I,
K). A sign: one decision shown, many relations held. Provenance of
the mark: MINTED, provisional until knolled.*

## Pin

Semiotics is the stratum where meaning gets a visible body — the sign
systems. It is the one stratum that is already **real and running**:
`glyphs/` holds two letter systems sharing the 27-cell solid (one
naming *addresses*, one naming *occupants*, both leaving the center
letterless), the letterform readings, the Core 27, all derived from
the vault by `knoll.py` and re-validated on every run. The grip
discipline — pin, non-example, provenance status — is this stratum's
native law, and the strata files in this directory inherit it.

## Creole reading

This is the stratum where the lexifier relationship is most literal
(`docs/sources/rosetta-creole.md`, cited as inspiration): the Latin
alphabet is a parent vocabulary, and the creole assigns the borrowed
letterforms its own addresses and occupancy — same 26 letters, new
grammar. The alphabet arrives flat ("Q doesn't know it lives near R,"
`docs/phase-2/cube-alphabet.md:52`); ontum's usage gives it
adjacency, type, and orientation — exactly the structure a creole
imposes on borrowed words.

## Non-example

An icon chosen for looks — a flame emoji on a dashboard — is not an
ontum sign. It has no address, no occupancy, no pin, no provenance
line. Decoration that cannot be placed in the solid or traced to a
source is styling, not semiotics.
