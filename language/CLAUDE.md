# language/ — the creole's strata

The home of ontum as a language surface: the living third grammar
between deterministic code (Software 1.0) and model inference
(Software 3.0). The frame is the rosetta-creole reading
(`docs/sources/rosetta-creole.md` — inspiration, not claim: every
stratum here pins to the phase-2 vault and to what is real on disk,
and only *cites* the source).

[strata.md](strata.md) is the spine — the four strata and the
traversal that orders them. One file per stratum:
[syntax.md](syntax.md), [semantics.md](semantics.md),
[semiotics.md](semiotics.md), [pragmatics.md](pragmatics.md). Each
holds a **pin** (what the stratum is here), the **creole reading**,
**holdings** (what is real on disk today), a **proposed mark**, a
**non-example**, and **named holes** — the grip discipline at stratum
scale.

Rules of this directory:

- **Pins cite the vault; the vault stays read-only.** A claim about
  the geometry quotes `docs/phase-2/` with its line. An inconsistency
  found there becomes a recorded finding, never a fix.
- **Marks here are proposed, not minted.** `glyphs/registry.json` is
  generated from the vault by `knoll.py` and never hand-edited; a
  stratum mark graduates only through the glyph-knolling ritual and
  bdo's admission. Until then its provenance line says so.
- **Open beats invented.** A stratum whose answer does not exist yet
  (pragmatics, today) holds the question with status OPEN. Absence is
  information (hard rule).
- Provenance legend follows the knolling: **PINNED / DERIVED /
  MINTED / OPEN**.
