## Pin

Semantics is where meaning lives and how it becomes comparable. In
ontum, meaning is local first: the **stalk** — intrinsic content with
dynamics, not frozen data (`docs/phase-2/autojective-polysheaf.md:37`).
A token's universal meaning is never authored; it is induced from
every Site it participates in — local frame vs universal frame
(`autojective-polysheaf.md:64`). And the join is where the meaning
concentrates: a Seam carries a comparison struct, not a boolean
(`autojective-polysheaf.md:29`); "a Seam that does not close is not a
bug; it is where the system's most informative content sits"
(`autojective-polysheaf.md:39`).

Comparability is not free. Load-bearing commitment 1: **there is a
metric**, and for semantics it is *manufactured* — aggregation plus
LLM conversion is the measurement instrument
(`autojective-polysheaf.md:88`).

## Creole reading

The membrane reading (`docs/sources/rosetta-creole.md`, cited as
inspiration): the seam is where Software 1.0 and 3.0 negotiate, and a
mismatch is measured as obstruction data — "leakage" — rather than
crashing (1.0's move) or drifting past (3.0's move). The source's own
caution, kept: a leakage *number* without an admitted metric reports
obstruction where there is none. If a seam metric is ever built, it
is an admitted record signed `--by`, never a constant in code.

## Holdings (real on disk today)

- The relational skeleton: the knolling's `seam of` columns — which
  cell is the seam of which — derived and re-validated by `knoll.py`.
- Nothing executable. No stalk has a value; no seam computes a
  comparison. The metric exists as commitment, not instrument.

## Non-example

A glossary entry is not ontum semantics. A definition is prose about
a term; a stalk is content with dynamics under a metric that makes it
comparable to its neighbors. Meaning that cannot be compared across a
seam has no semantic standing here, however well written.
