An anthology is the **arc-tier fold** — a derived
envelope over several arcs, re-deriving like arcs into one body of work whose
derived type is its *throughline* (see `session-gateway.proposal.md` and the
registered memory `ontum-session-gateway-governed-folding`). Naming and
blessing an anthology is **bdo's** (D-4); a session may only *propose* one.
This file is that proposal. The part that would fold it deterministically
(`loop/anthology.py`, sibling of `digest`/`retro`/`heal`) is a later
increment; until then this proposal *is* the draft record, and the bless can
be an admission (`confirm-arc`-shaped) when the part exists.

## Why now

The 2026-06-18 session-gateway thread landed as **~6 PRs scattered across three
confirmed arcs** (`substrate`, `the-field`, `owner-harness`). They are one
throughline wearing three arc-labels. That scatter is the WIP-sprawl the
anthology concept exists to cure: aggregate-backpressure and steering at the
arc tier, so bdo steers *one* body of work, not six PRs across three arcs — the
same lift as done-line 0028 (piece→arc), moved up one level (arc→anthology).

## Proposed throughline

> **The loop governs, provisions, and staffs itself safely** — a session is
> insulated (its own branch/workspace) but never isolated (always a route home:
> provision in, merge-node to land, gardener home), every act is authorized
> (rung/D-4/no-self-sign) and attributed (a signed receipt), and "landed" means
> independently reviewed, never self-signed.

Candidate names (bdo's to set): `anthology.self-governing-loop`,
`anthology.session-gateway`, `anthology.insulated-not-isolated`.

## What bless means

Blessing this anthology: (1) sets its **name**, (2) fixes its **boundary**
(which chapters are in), and (3) authorizes steering it as one unit. It does
**not** re-confirm the member arcs (each is already confirmed) and it does
**not** land anything — the merge-node still lands each PR. It is a *steering*
record, not a landing one.
