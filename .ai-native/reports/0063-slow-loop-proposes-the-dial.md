## The resolution (propose vs dispose)

The contradiction dissolves into two halves. The loop may **propose** a setpoint
change that is *caused by outcomes* and carries its attribution — that is the
causal content; a cycle with no attributable cause is repetition, not a causal
loop. The **disposition** — the act of admitting the change, of moving what the
system aims at — stays an act of the **outside**. The proposer is built and is
answer-agnostic. The loop is now closed enough to be causal and open exactly
where an outside is required. Every surface honours the split: even *showing* the
proposal is kept to sessions that cannot act on it, so visibility never becomes
routing.

## needs-you

One genuine gesture, and it is the seam the whole arc was built to expose: the
**disposition** of a slow-loop proposal. Who, or what, admits it? Three shapes,
all consistent with D-4, the choice yours:

  1. **you forever** — proposals always wait for bdo's --admit-setpoint. The loop
     stays open at the dial by design; safest, slowest.
  2. **a bounded standing auto-admit** — like arc-confirm: you stamp limits once
     (e.g. step_budget within [2,5], cooling always allowed, heating capped), and
     proposals inside the bounds self-admit, the rest escalate. The loop closes
     within a fence you drew.
  3. **an independent judge node** — a node that is not the proposer admits
     within a policy, the way the merge-node lands what it did not author. The
     outside becomes a second agent, not you.

Nothing is wired toward any of these — that waits on your answer, because the
wiring *is* the answer.
