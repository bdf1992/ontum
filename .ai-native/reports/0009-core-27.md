## The mint

**Core 27** — every glyph of the 27, opened as its own local frame,
occupies that frame's void: the keystone, the Self/void term. Globally a
cell among cells; locally the center of its own neighborhood. The 27
glyphs (26 letters + ⊘) are therefore the system's core operational
terms — not just an alphabet but an inventory of Selves, each anchoring a
frame.

- **Coining utterance recorded** in the registry (`core27.coined_by`),
  etymology-style — the source event of the term is inspectable.
- **Non-example proposed** (so the grip holds a referent, not a hole):
  cant, drift, jective — operational vocabulary, but not core-27; they
  have no cell and anchor no local frame.
- **The openness gradient**, derived and tested: corner frames hold 7
  neighbors (19 slots open), edges 11/15, faces 17/9, ⊘ 26/0. The root
  closes completely — its frame is the whole specimen. The open-slot
  counts are exactly the capacity each core term has for *future*
  placements: corners are the most open (19 holes each), the void has
  none. Where new terms/glyphs can land is now a number, not a vibe.
