# Done-line 0114 — The healing fold — the loop senses where its own teeth bit too sharp

Written before code, per §9.4. When this line is met, stop.

The diagnosis (this session, 2026-06-17, with bdo): the loop is rich in teeth — five gates that can refuse, a placement gate that catches collisions, a value-confirm that caught a phantom delivery. But a crawl of the whole record shows the gates almost never refuse (≈3.4% of all verdicts are non-advancing), and the one place they *did* bite repeatedly, they bit badly: `atom.gates-enumerated` was sent back by the handoff gate on v2 and waved through on v3 — the bar flapped — and `atom.field-topology.v0` still surfaces as a parked refusal on the owner views long after v1 built the thing and `confirmed`. Teeth without a healing reflex is autoimmunity: the system attacks healthy tissue (churn) and keeps a correct-but-stale bite inflamed on the surfaces bdo reads. bdo's direction: before sharpening any tooth, build the counterforce — an organ that senses where a tooth was *too sharp* or has gone *stale*, and proposes the heal. Heal first; teeth-placement is the named-next.

The frame (no double-build, §10; read-only, D-4): heal reads truth, it is not a second one. It folds the same append-only log every other organ folds (`loop.reconcile.Fold`) and *reuses* the version-split (`loop.digest._base_version`), the realness map (`loop.reconcile.real_nodes`), and supersession (`loop.reconcile.superseded_atom_ids`) rather than re-deriving any of them. Its new lens is **the bite**: a refusal read not as backpressure-working but as a candidate over-reach, surfaced only when the record itself contradicts the bite. Evidence is cited to receipt ids (the grip rule). It is propose-only: it names the over-bite and the one heal move; it never clears a park, re-opens a verdict, edits the log, or admits anything (a future increment may earn a *bounded* actuator through the disposer fence — not this one). Sibling of `retro` on the bite axis: retro asks "what keeps happening", heal asks "where did a tooth bite wrong".

> **Done when:** `loop/heal.py` is a pure, stdlib, read-only fold over the entire log that surfaces *over-bites* as findings, each carrying `kind · subject · cited receipt evidence · the one heal move` (the gaps/retro grain). It implements at least three detectors — **flapping-gate** (the same real gate returns a non-advancing verdict on one version of a base and an advancing verdict on another version — the bar moved without the work failing consistently), **stale-park** (a refused version that a later version *passed at the same gate* superseded — a correct bite whose park still surfaces), and **owner-override** (bdo's owner-stamp advanced a base a real gate had refused — the owner overruled the tooth; declared even at zero live instances, like the cool valve). `python -m loop.heal` renders a scannable read and ends `done | report`; `--json` emits the dataset. The ambient surface receives the upgrade: `loop.summon`'s hook gains a read-only `heal_lines` line (the slowloop-line pattern — shown, never disposed) so every session that blinks in feels the over-bite backlog. A §10 test (`tests/test_heal.py`) joins the suite with teeth: it plants a flapping pattern (same gate, send_back then advance on adjacent versions) and asserts the fold catches it; plants a *consistently* refused atom and asserts it is NOT flagged as flapping (a correct, repeated bite is not an over-bite); plants a stale-park (refuse v0, pass v1) and asserts catch, and a genuine open park (refuse v0, no later pass) and asserts NO flag; and fails a fabricated/constant classifier (a detector that returns findings without resolving real receipts). The full suite is green and the work is a PR the merge-node can land.

> **Non-example:** any write path — clearing a park, re-opening a verdict, auto-superseding, or admitting a re-calibration (heal proposes; the actuator, if it ever comes, rides the disposer's bounded fence and is a later done-line, not this one); flagging a refusal as an over-bite on a vibe rather than a record that contradicts the bite (the §10 test exists to kill exactly that — a consistently-refused atom must read as good backpressure, never as a too-sharp tooth); re-deriving the version-split, realness, supersession, or divergence math instead of reusing the existing folds (no second truth); regexing report prose for "teeth that felt too sharp" (evidence is receipts, not sentences); or claiming this increment heals anything — it *senses and proposes*; the heal stays a session's or bdo's move (D-4).
