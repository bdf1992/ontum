### Charge — derived from binary (dual-rail), not asserted

Two rails (+rail, −rail), each on/off. Cite: **Null Convention Logic** (dual-rail

| (+rail, −rail) | charge | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| (0,0) | **Null** | chargeless — empty/spacer state (the *potential*) |
| (1,0) | **+** | positive |
| (0,1) | **−** | negative |
| (1,1) | **+−** | both rails — quantum (superposed) / paradox (forced collapse) |
| *computed* | **0** | **neutral** — conserved sum of a chain whose charges cancel |

- **Null ≠ 0:** Null is *empty* (nothing set); 0 is *filled but balanced* — the
  self's empty/filled line, in bits.
- **0 is computed, not atomic** — the conservation law (cf. `colorchain.neutral()`).
- **anti-charge** = swap rails (+ ↔ −); Null and +− self-conjugate (charge-conj C).
- **Charge is per-axis (a vector), not a scalar.** Each axis carries its own
  dual-rail charge; **isocharge** = the charge on a *sibling* axis (cf. weak
  **isospin**, SU(2) — read-only prior). *Null* means null *on this axis*, not
  globally inert.
- **The neutrino case:** electric = **Null**, but **isocharge ≠ 0** and it is
  chiral — repelled/anti in the *iso* axis and by its handedness, not the electric
  one. Its anti (antineutrino) = a **chirality/spin flip**, not a charge-sign flip.
  So **anti can come from spin**, not only charge-conjugation.
- **Null is doubly-absent: ¬have ∧ ¬want.** No charge *and* no affinity — inert,
  no force. **Affinity (want/acquire?) is a second dial**, orthogonal to value:
  - ¬have ∧ ¬want = **Null** (inert, dead-empty, no force)
  - ¬have ∧ want = **vacancy / hole** — active absence that *pulls* (emits the
    felt force; cf. semiconductor holes — read-only prior)
  - have ∧ ¬want = **saturated** (stable, content)
  - have ∧ want = **acquisitive** (accreting, unstable)
  - The **felt force** comes from *affinity* (wanting), not from mere absence.
- This splits **empty**: *dead-empty* (Null) vs *hungry-empty* (a vacancy = the
  **potential that wants to become** — latent seeking actual). The self's "empty"
  is the hungry kind.

## The bicone — the continuous half of a binary axis

The bicone **falls out the way color did** — color was the *discrete* half of the

- two **cone tips** = the discrete poles (±);

  pole→neck;
- the **neck** = the neutral center (0), the σ-fixed observer.

So **"rail = direction + amount" *is* a bicone** (direction picks the pole, amount =
position along the cone). The three handles:

2. **R and angle** — the gradient's opening;
3. **cone ∩ plane** — the **conic sections** (circle/ellipse/parabola/hyperbola) =

- **toroidal bound → braids & knots** (torus knots; the braid group in the solid torus).

Bicone + tori-bound holds topology, geometry, algebra, calculus, monoids, braids,

The coil holds **energy**; **mass** is computed from it; both are read against **C**.

**What pins C? (OPEN — your call.)** Candidates:

- **The massless class** — a coil with *zero* stored energy/inductance propagates at C.
  So C = the no-mass propagation rate (physics: c = speed of the massless).

  be its tick/clock (how fast the render updates from the observer).
- **The proton (your guess)** most naturally pins the **mass unit/scale** (Λ_QCD ≈ the
  confinement scale from the strong-force card), *not* C directly — but with a chosen
  energy unit it ties to C via `E = Mc²`. So: **proton → mass scale; masslessness → C.**

**C is set at the configuration event (the "big bang").** Before the config there is
no constant — flux, fields **decoupled**. This *is* the pre-config bedrock (gen ≤0,
mythos) and it explains *why* bedrock is **uncomputable: no C exists yet.** The config

**The config can reconfigure faster than C.** C bounds motion *through* the configured

**Density slows the effective C** (it is "through a vacuum"). Vacuum C is the max;

slower — which is **mass-as-drag** (the coil's inductance) *and* **refraction** at once.
Dense regions slow the read (gravity-like). So C is a **vacuum reference**; the
**effective** rate is **local**, set by what you read through.

The calculation (`M = E/c²`) is settled; C is **defined by the configuration** (gen 0)
and its **effective value is local** (density-modulated).

## Forces — tension out of bounds, relieved by splitting

A **force** is what happens when the **difference (tension)** between two related,
bounded things exceeds a **bound**. The response **relieves** the tension — and nature
**splits / creates**, it does not break.

- **Tension** = the field-difference between two related things (color, spin, sign, …),
  which is **stored energy** (the stretched coil / inductance). Tension = the field-stack
  comparison = mass/energy.
- **Out of bounds → a force acts**, and *which* force = **which field's bound broke**:
  - **color** out of bounds → **strong** → the color field **splits**: stored energy
    converts to a new quark–antiquark **pair** (E = Mc²), giving two color-neutral
    pieces. *Create, not break* (the confinement mechanic).
  - **flavor/type** out of bounds → **weak** → flavor change (the W); the difference is
    carried away by emitted particles (the receipt).
  - **charge** difference → **EM** → attract / repel.
  - **mass / density** → **gravity** (slows effective C).
- **The color field needs a SPLIT operation** — pair creation: stretched past its bound,
  mint a new color+anticolor pair so every piece stays neutral.

**How the relief actually works (graded, not a snap):**

1. **Offset** — the coil **redistributes its lumps** along itself to oppose the tension
   (**inductive opposition — Lenz's law:** a coil resists a change). The elastic phase;
   nothing breaks, lumps just move to absorb the stress.
2. **Generate** — when the offset is exhausted (out of bounds), separation **generates
   energy of a specific *type*** — the **force-carrier** of that field (strong → gluon,
   EM → photon, weak → W/Z). The energy's type *is* the field whose bound broke.
3. **Attract** — that typed energy condenses into a **specific color pair** (color +
   anticolor → neutral): new mass/energy that arrives *already bound* (E → Mc²),
   carrying its own attraction. The pairing rule is **color-neutralization.**

Both the energy generated and the attraction-pair are **typed by which field's tension
broke.** So forces = **bound-restoring tension-relief**: **lumps move (offset) → typed
energy is generated → a bound pair arrives** (energy → mass at the bound). The dynamics
layer.
