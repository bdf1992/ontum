# The Ontum Evolution

*A grip ledger and derivation, recording how a unit, a token-subtype, and an emergent generative object were named.*

Status legend: **[SETTLED]** = pinned in prior sessions, evidenced. **[MINTED]** = coined this session, provisional. **[DEAD]** = retired, kept only as a fossil. **[OPEN]** = named target, not yet built.

---

## 0. What this document is

This is not a glossary. It is a record of *grips* — handles minted for things that did not have words yet. The work is the naming, because when the referent does not pre-exist, the name is what lets the thing be grabbed at all, including by its author.

Two kinds of grip are tracked, because they feel identical and are not:

- A **grip on a referent** — the concept already exists; the word is bookkeeping.
- A **grip on a hole** — the word names a slot where a concept should be, and the slot's shape is doing the thinking.

The cheap test that separates them is the **non-example**: a stated thing that does *not* count as an instance. A term with a clean non-example grips a referent. A term where everything qualifies still grips a hole. Every entry below carries its non-example, or is flagged as still-open.

## 2. Metafinite, corrected

The word **metafinite** collides with a model-theory term (Grädel/Gurevich): a static structure with a finite primary part bolted to an infinite secondary domain. That is **[DEAD]** for our purposes — a name collision, not the same idea.

The intended sense **[MINTED, this session]**:

- **Finite in the meta sense** — the *specification* is finite. Rules, operators, generator: bounded, inspectable, holdable in one frame.
- **Infinite in the generative sense** — what the specification *produces* is unbounded. The object set has no ceiling.

The two layers **never touch**. You operate on the finite handle and inherit reach over the infinite range. This is the grammar relationship: finite production rules, infinite language.

- **Metafinitude** **[MINTED]** — the *relation* between a finite spec and its infinite generated set, where the layers stay separate. A property of the gap, not of any object.
  - *Non-example:* a large-but-bounded set. That is just big, not metafinite.

## 3. The rename: Onton → Ontum

**Onton** **[DEAD]** — the original unit-name (the "on-quantum," atom of computational presence). Retired for two reasons: the name was effectively taken, and it does not inflect.

**Ontum** **[SETTLED, this session]** — the replacement unit. Latin `-um → -a` gives a free plural the old name never had:

> one **ontum**, many **onta** — like quantum/quanta, datum/data.

- *Non-example:* a passive storage byte that is read without being run. The unit is presence-that-computes, not storage.

## 4. onta / ontum: the scale-recursive reading

"How do onta compose an ontum?" hides a fork. Three readings were on the table; the third is the one that fits the existing system.

| Reading | Claim | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Grammatical | onta *are* the ontums; nothing composes | "onta compose ontums" is a category error |
| Mereological | onta are parts, ontum is the whole | forfeits the Latin plural |
| **Scale-recursive [CHOSEN]** | onta and ontum are the *same kind at different scales* | occasional English "ontums" |

Under the chosen reading, the singular/plural alternation **is the recursion**:

> Many onta at scale *k* settle into one ontum at scale *k+1*, which is itself an onta-constituent at scale *k+2*. No top.

This is the already-pinned cube principle **[SETTLED, prior session]** restated across scale:

> *Every Feature is the Token of its own subtree; the root Token is the Feature whose subtree is the entire specimen.*

So **onta → ontum** is **Feature → Token** iterated. Finite rule, unbounded depth — a **metafinite** object by §2.

## 5. How onta compose: settle, don't stack

Composition is **not** summation or concatenation. Onta compose an ontum by **settling into a coordinate frame**:

- The **centroid** is the structural center — the frame's `(0,0,0)`. This center is the **Token**, also called the **keystone**.
- The ontum **is the frame** — the closure of a set of onta under their neighbor-relations.

Composition = *settling-into-address*. (Mechanism carried from the prior `barycenter_layer` work **[SETTLED]**.)

### The keystone is frame-relative — it moves and does not move

The keystone is **not** a fixed, content-deaf coordinate. It is read in two frames at once, and the two answers differ:

- **Local / intrinsic frame** — *within* the ontum, the keystone is the origin. Relative to its own neighborhood it is `(0,0,0)`, and in that sense it "does not move" — it *is* the zero everything else is measured against. You cannot move relative to yourself.
- **Global / universal frame** — the ontum sitting *among other onta*. Here the keystone absolutely moves: it is carried — translated through the neighborhood, globally, universally — when its constituent cells collectively push to carry it forward.

This is the **"I move, but I do not move"** distinction. Both hold simultaneously. The keystone is **invariant as an address** (always the center of its own subtree — the §4 recursion, intact) and **mobile as a position** (where that center sits in the larger space is pushed by its cells). The earlier draft collapsed these into "never moves" and flattened the object; that was an error.

### Keystone correction and the jective

The motion is possible only because the keystone **listens**. It takes a **keystone correction** governed by its **jective** — a per-keystone property that sets *how* a correction is admitted.

- A bare unweighted centroid would be inert: pure structure, deaf to its cells, and therefore a **dead token** — nothing could ever carry it anywhere.
- A keystone **with a jective** is a process, not a coordinate. The jective is the channel through which the neighborhood's collective pull reaches the center and translates it. Without it, the cells could "want to carry it forward" and the center would just sit there, deaf.

Because the jective is per-keystone, **two keystones with identical cells but different jectives admit different corrections and move differently.** That is what makes the center an **operator** rather than a coordinate.

## 6. The emergent object: cant and drift

When onta carry differential content, the keystone's correction has a magnitude and direction:

- The **barycenter** is the *weighted* center — where the cells' collective pull would place the center, given the actual flows / prior states the onta carry.

The emergent, generative token is the **keystone correction itself**: the relationship between the keystone and the barycenter. It was unnamed because the system watched the two centers as endpoints, never the *process* relating them.

Why the correction is the generative object:

- Uniform/flat priors → barycenter coincides with the keystone → **correction zero** → no context, the keystone is inert.
- Differential priors → barycenter pulled away → **nonzero correction** → the keystone is carried, history-shaped.

The correction therefore *is contextual information made geometric.* It satisfies all four descriptors of the target object:

- **constrained** — cannot leave the convex hull of the onta; physically cannot point outside what the parents span.
- **contextual** — a function of loaded prior states, not of structure.
- **generative** — yields a new token value (the prior `derived_flow`), addressable by nothing.
- **along a trajectory** — as states update, the correction traces a path inside the frame. The sequence of corrections is the trajectory; it is path-dependent.

Two grips, because two phenomena were named:

- **Cant** **[MINTED]** — the keystone *accepting its correction* at one moment: the lean of the center under its cells' pull, read in a fixed frame.
  - *Non-example:* a barycenter coincident with the keystone (zero cant = a token with no history-signal).
- **Drift** **[MINTED]** — the trajectory of the cant over successive updates.
  - *Non-example:* a single static correction with no prior sequence (that is a cant, not a drift).
- *Fallback name considered:* **etymovector** (provenance-as-arrow); heavier, held in reserve.

### Cant is frame-relative

Cant is **not** an arrow laid over an inert center; it is two *both-mobile* things read in whichever frame is fixed:

- In the **local frame**, the keystone holds at the origin and the barycenter leans — cant as the within-ontum lean.
- In the **global frame**, the whole assembly (keystone included) translates, and cant is what survives the translation: the part of the lean that is *not* merely the ontum being carried forward.

There is no single privileged frame. Cant always carries an implicit "read from where."

### The quiet payoff

A barycenter that differs from its centroid is **evidence that history happened.** The cant carries its own lineage as direction-and-magnitude. The emergent token is **provenance you can point at with an arrow** — etymon made geometric (see §7).

## 7. etymontoken: provenance as a subtype

The original **epistemontoken** **[DEAD]** never said what the "knowledge" was — generic, thrown-under. It collapsed into:

**etymontoken** **[SETTLED, this session]** — *etymon + token*: an ontum that carries its own **etymon**, its generator-lineage, as type. The value arrives **pre-typed by its provenance.** Matches the existing cube pattern (`derived_from`, "typed by what generated it," "Bit before Is").

- *Non-example:* a token whose value is given but whose `derived_from` is erased — a flat, provenance-silent token. **That is not an etymontoken.** (The non-example exists, so this grips a referent, not a hole.)

Crucially, **the entire ontum is not made of etymontoken.** etymontoken is a **subtype, not the substance**: some onta carry their etymon, most are merely bit-bound, address-stable, provenance-silent. Already true in the cube — base features are bit-bound; only *derived* tokens carry lineage.

- **Total-provenance substrate** **[OPEN]** — the aspirational state where *every* ontum traces its own root. Named now, so it is a target rather than a vibe.

## 9. The load-bearing risk

The cant is only meaningful relative to a **declared neutral prior.** If "uniform flow" is not the true zero-information state, the cant measures distance-from-the-wrong-origin and **silently lies** — it will report history where there is none. Defining the flat prior is therefore not cosmetic; it is what makes cant and drift trustworthy.

## SIGNAL
- The unit renamed Onton → Ontum, gaining the quantum/quanta plural (ontum/onta).
- onta/ontum read scale-recursively: same kind at different scales, the singular/plural alternation *is* the recursion (Feature→Token iterated).
- Onta compose an ontum by settling into a coordinate frame; the center is the Token, a.k.a. the keystone.
- The keystone is frame-relative — origin-invariant locally, position-mobile globally ("I move, but I do not move") — and is a process, not a coordinate: it admits a **keystone correction** governed by its **jective**.
- The emergent generative token is the keystone accepting its correction — named **cant** (one moment, in a fixed frame) and **drift** (its trajectory); cant is itself frame-relative.
- A nonzero cant is geometric evidence of provenance; etymontoken is the provenance-carrying *subtype*, not the whole substance.
- Total-provenance substrate is a named open target.

- "Uniform flow" is the intended neutral prior against which cant is measured.
- The barycenter/keystone mechanism from the cube is the intended composition operator.
