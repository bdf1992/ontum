# docs/culture/ — the authored telling of the goal

This directory is **authored material, not read-only context** — the one
place in `docs/` a session may write. It exists because the doctrine
(`ai-native-loop-substrate.md`, §15–§16 and its v0.4.0 changelog) defers
"the full telling" of *what this is all for* to
`docs/culture/the-idea.md` and `docs/culture/the-ambient-loop.md`, and
that pointer was dangling — the files did not exist (the absence is
recorded in `docs/phase-2/README.md` and was the gap
[report 0004](../../.ai-native/reports/0004-the-frame-harness-and-fabric.md)
named).

Distinct from its neighbours under `docs/`:

- `docs/phase-2/` and `docs/sources/` are **read-only context** — the
  fabric being built and external inspiration. The loop never edits them.
- `docs/culture/` (here) is **the goal narrative the repo authors about
  itself**: the complete telling of the horizon, synthesized from what is
  on the record (the doctrine, the epic horizons, the frame reports) and
  carried for a reader who needs the *why* before the *how*.

## Rules of this directory

- **Synthesis, never a second source of truth.** Everything here is a
  *fold of the record told as prose* — it cites the doctrine, the epic
  horizons (`python -m loop.node arcs`), the phase-2 vault, and the
  numbered reports. When the record and a sentence here disagree, the
  record wins and the sentence is the bug.
- **The goal is the fabric, never the harness.** The category error
  report 0004 caught — describing the loop substrate as "what ontum is
  trying to be" — is the one this directory exists to keep corrected. The
  loop is the cycle; Ontum is what runs on it.
- **Provisional until stamped.** A telling of bdo's north star is his to
  shape. A file here authored by a session carries a provisional mark
  until bdo confirms it; absence and uncertainty are named, never
  papered over (the grip discipline — carry a non-example or be flagged a
  hole).
