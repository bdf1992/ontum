# DEPOSITS — taste, lifted to intent (refusal deposits)

*The experience rule: praise deposits, refusal deposits too. bdo's
corrections on the v1 portfolio's first render (2026-06-16), lifted to
intent (never verbatim) — register-level craft, belongs in the editorial
family. Each is a bar the next render must clear.*

## R1 · Light is earned, dark is not a default
**Refusal:** "Too dark."
**Intent:** A register's base value must serve readability and life, not
mood for its own sake. Darkness is load-bearing only when the content
demands it (a star map, a threat globe); a reading-and-judging surface
earns a luminous ground. Defaulting to near-black because the foundry's
showcases are dark is habit, not craft.
**Clears when:** the ground is light/warm, high-contrast, and nothing
reads as black-on-black.

## R2 · Readability is the floor beneath "typography leads"
**Refusal:** "Poor typography readability with everything considered."
**Intent:** Type hierarchy is not craft if it isn't legible first.
Ultra-tracked tiny grey caps and grey-on-grey body fail the floor before
any hierarchy is even read. Contrast and size come before style; the
micro-label aesthetic cannot cost legibility.
**Clears when:** body and labels are comfortably readable at a glance,
real contrast, a hierarchy that reads across the room (typography-leads)
WITHOUT sacrificing the floor.

## R3 · Pacing is composition, not placement
**Refusal:** "Arbitrary decoration pacing."
**Intent:** Vertical rhythm and the cadence of decorative elements
(labels, rules, dividers, whitespace) must sit on a composed scale, felt
as intentional. Ad-hoc spacing reads as scatter even when each piece is
nice. The rhythm is a system, not a series of one-off gaps.
**Clears when:** spacing follows a scale and the decoration cadence is
deliberate — the eye feels a meter.

## R4 · Depth is the line between a document and an experience
**Refusal:** "Flat experience."
**Intent:** Flat panels on a flat ground read as a document. An
experience has dimension — elevation, layering, motion-in-depth, an
arrival that builds the world rather than printing it. Kin to
the-arrival and fixed-camera-moving-world.
**Clears when:** the surface has real depth (layered elevation, parallax
or motion-in-depth) and the arrival gives the world dimension.
